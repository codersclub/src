[Mini-digest: 6 responses]

This toolbar type is known as a "CoolBar" and is part of the next
generation of common controls. It supposedly will be officially released as
part of IE 4.0, whenever and whatever that turns out to be. As of today the
CoolBar (and the other nifty new controls) are "not officially released and
distributable." 

The above quote appears in the Oct. '96 issue of MSJ in an article by
Strohm Armstrong, "Previewing the New and Improved Common Controls DLL for
Microsoft IE 4.0"

They utilize the new COMCTL32.DLL, which has new styles and messages for
the toolbar class.  They have not yet been documented by Microsoft, and not
yet supported in MFC.  The answer is, RSN.

_VERY_ generally speaking, the Office guys end up pushing the UI
design envelope at Microsoft faster than other groups do.  They 
have the best base of "common" users to try for usability testing,
