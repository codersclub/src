What a coincidence, I just hit that the other day. As you've probably
noticed, that's a known design oddity in NT, and there is a
KnowledgeBase article explaining why. If you can't find it on
Microsoft's Web Site, I can try and locate it for you.

>>
>What I need know is if there is any way to get the "real" system time
>(32 bit) and not the one that the Virtual DOS Machine is using?
<<

The aforementioned KnowledgeBase article very strongly implies that you
can't. It says that starting up the VDM takes a "snapshot" of the
then-current system time, and there's no mechanism to update it.

In addition, *another* KnowledgeBase article states that if you change
the system time/date from within your 16-bit app, that change is also
not reflected up to the operating system, and only applies to your VDM
(or is it all running 16-bit apps?). Anyway, I can find that KB article
too if you need it.

If you *do* find a solution, please be sure to post it in the list
(and/or E-mail it to me). :-)
